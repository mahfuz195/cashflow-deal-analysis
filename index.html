Rental Property Investment Formulas

Cash Flow Formula
Monthly Cash Flow = (Monthly Rent × Units × (1 − Vacancy%)) − Monthly Expenses − Monthly Mortgage
Cap Rate Formula
Cap Rate = (Annual NOI / Purchase Price) × 100
Cash-on-Cash ROI Formula
CoC ROI = (Annual Cash Flow / Cash Invested) × 100
Net Operating Income (NOI) Formula
NOI = Effective Gross Income − Total Operating Expenses (excluding mortgage)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good cap rate for rental property?

In most US markets, a cap rate of 5–8% is considered good for residential rental properties. Hot markets like NYC or LA may see 3–5%, while Midwest markets can offer 8–12%. Cap rate = NOI / purchase price.

What is a good cash-on-cash return?

Most real estate investors target 8%+ CoC ROI as excellent, 5–8% as good, and anything below 0% as a deal to avoid. Cash-on-cash return measures your annual cash profit as a percentage of the cash you put in (down payment + closing costs).
